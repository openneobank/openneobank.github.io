Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 14, 2026

This policy covers the openneobank.io website and project — the pages you're reading now, not any bank or app a third party builds by self-hosting the open-source software. If you deploy Open Neobank yourself, you are the data controller for your own users, and this policy doesn't apply to your deployment. This is a starting template, not legal advice — have it reviewed for your jurisdiction before relying on it.

1. What this site collects

Browsing this site, we collect the same things most static sites do: standard server logs (IP address, browser type, pages visited, timestamps) used only to keep the site running and to understand aggregate traffic.

If you submit your email address to be notified about managed hosting, we store that email address and nothing else attached to it — no financial data, no account information, no tracking of what you do elsewhere.

2. What we don't collect

This website does not process banking transactions, account balances, or customer financial data. Nothing on openneobank.io asks for a card number, a government ID, or any information you'd hand to an actual bank. The account balances and card shown on this page are illustrative sample data, not real accounts.

3. How we use what we collect

  • To send you a single email when managed hosting becomes available, if you asked for one.
  • To keep aggregate, anonymized analytics on which pages and docs get used, so we know what to improve.
  • To keep the site secure and diagnose issues from server logs.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your email address or any other data to third parties.

4. Cookies

This site does not use advertising or cross-site tracking cookies. Any cookies present are strictly functional (for example, remembering that you dismissed a banner) or used for privacy-respecting, aggregate analytics.

5. Third parties

We rely on standard infrastructure providers (hosting, email delivery, analytics) to run this site. Those providers process data only as needed to provide their service to us, under their own privacy commitments.

6. Your choices

You can ask us to delete any email address we hold for you at any time — see the contact section below. There's nothing else to opt out of, since we don't build a profile beyond that.

7. If you self-host Open Neobank

The open-source project gives you a ledger, payments adapters, and a white-label app — but privacy compliance for the bank you build (KYC data, transaction records, disclosures to your own end users) is your responsibility as the operator. This policy does not, and cannot, cover deployments we don't run.

8. Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we'll update the date at the top of the page. Material changes will be called out on the site.

9. Contact

Questions about this policy or a data request can be sent to privacy@openneobank.io.
